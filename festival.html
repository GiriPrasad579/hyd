1.Bathukamma

Bathukamma is a vibrant floral festival predominantly celebrated in Telangana, India, and some parts of Andhra Pradesh, honoring the inherent relationship between earth, water, and humanity.

It's a nine-day festival where women create beautiful floral stacks, call ed Bathukammas, and immerse them in water bodies, symbolizing nature's bounty and feminine power.

It's celebrated during the latter half of the monsoon season, before the onset of winter, usually coinciding with the Hindu month of Ashwayuja (September-October)

he festival is marked by women gathering in large numbers, dancing, singing traditional songs, and offering prayers around the Bathukammas.

3.Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is a vibrant Hindu festival of lights, typically celebrated over five days in the autumn months.

It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance

colorful rangoli patterns and traditional clay lamps called diyas, exchanging gifts and sweets, and enjoying fireworks
