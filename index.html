HYDERABAD LIFE OF TELANGANA

     Charminar, an iconic monument and mosque in Hyderabad, India is renowned for its architecture and historical significance. Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of a plague, it stands as a testament to Indo-Islamic design with Persian influences. The structure features four grand arches, each with a minaret risin g to a height of 48.7 meters, adorned with intricate carvings and stucco work.

 Key features of Charminar: Four Minarets: The monument's most striking feature is its four towering minarets, one at each corner, each with a spiral staircase leading to multiple levels and balconies offering city views. Arches and Mosque: Charminar also boasts four grand arches and a mosque on the top floor, along with 45 prayer spaces and an open area for worshippers. Architectural Style: It showcases Indo-Islamic architecture with Persian influences, evident in the arches, domes, and intricate detailing. Historical Significance: Built to celebrate the end of a plague, Charminar holds deep historical and cultural importance for Hyderabad. Location: Situated in the heart of Hyderabad, it's surrounded by bustling markets like Laad Bazaar, and is close to other historical landmarks like Mecca Masjid. Material: The monument is constructed from granite and lime mortar, with stucco ornamentation.
